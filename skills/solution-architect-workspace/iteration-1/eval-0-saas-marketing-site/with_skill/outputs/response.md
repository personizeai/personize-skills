# Personalization Proposal: SaaS Marketing Website

You have a strong foundation: 53 solution pages, a blog, landing pages, signup tracking, page views, and HubSpot CRM data. Right now every visitor sees the same static copy regardless of who they are. Here is how Personize changes that -- not by swapping a CTA color or inserting a first name, but by having AI **write original page copy, CTAs, and follow-up content** for each visitor using everything you know about them, governed by your brand rules.

Before diving in, let me be clear about what I'm NOT proposing. Showing "Hi {first_name}" on a page, swapping a banner based on industry segment, or recommending blog posts with a collaborative filter -- those are template logic any CRM plus a few if/else statements can handle. You don't need Personize for that. What follows are proposals where AI **generates** original content that could not exist without all three layers working together.

---

## How the Three Layers Apply to Your Site

Every proposal below uses all three layers. Here is what each one contributes in your context:

| Layer | What It Does For You | Without It |
|---|---|---|
| **Governance** (`smartGuidelines`) | Your brand voice, approved claims per industry vertical, competitor mention policy, compliance rules, messaging playbook per ICP | AI invents its own tone on every page. One solution page sounds formal, another sounds casual. Claims get made that marketing never approved. |
| **Unified Memory** (`smartDigest` + `recall`) | Combines HubSpot CRM data (company, role, deal stage, lifecycle) + page view history + signup data + any enrichment -- into one context window | AI sees only one data source. It knows the visitor's company name but not that they viewed 4 solution pages about API integrations and attended your webinar last week. |
| **Content Generation** (`prompt` with `instructions[]`) | Multi-step AI that WRITES original headlines, value props, CTAs, and page narratives unique to this visitor | You are doing conditional rendering with extra steps -- picking from pre-written variants instead of generating unique copy. |

## Proposal 1: AI-Generated Solution Page Copy Per Visitor

### The Experience

A visitor lands on one of your 53 solution pages. Instead of static copy that reads the same for everyone, the headline, value proposition, use case example, and CTA are **generated by AI** for this specific visitor -- referencing their industry, company size, role, where they are in the buying journey, and which other pages they have already read.

### Why It Needs All Three Layers

- **Governance:** Your messaging playbook defines approved claims per industry, brand voice, what proof points to use for enterprise vs. SMB, and what you can and cannot say about competitors.
- **Memory:** HubSpot tells you the visitor's company and role. Page view tracking tells you they already read 3 other solution pages (so the copy should build on that context, not repeat the same intro). Signup data tells you their stated goals. Combined, the AI sees the full picture.
- **Generation:** The AI WRITES a unique value proposition paragraph and CTA. Not "pick from variant A/B/C" but original copy that connects this visitor's situation to this specific solution page's content.

### Before (What You Have Now)

Every visitor to your "API Integration" solution page sees:

> "Seamless API Integrations for Modern Teams. Connect your tools and automate workflows with our powerful API platform."

### After (Three-Layer Personalization)

A VP of Engineering at a 200-person fintech company who already viewed your Security and Compliance pages sees:

> "API Integrations Built for Regulated Environments. Your engineering team at [Company] is scaling integrations across a compliance-heavy stack -- and based on your interest in our security architecture, you care about doing it right. Here is how teams in financial services connect 50+ tools through our API while maintaining audit trails and SOC2 compliance. [See the Fintech Integration Playbook]"

A marketing ops manager at a 30-person SaaS startup who arrived from a blog post about automation sees:

> "Connect HubSpot, Salesforce, and 40+ Tools Without Engineering Help. You are running lean -- and the last thing your team needs is a 3-month integration project. Here is how marketing teams like yours automate cross-tool workflows in days, not quarters. [Start Your Free Integration]"

### Technical Pattern

```typescript
import { Personize } from '@personize/sdk';
const client = new Personize({ secretKey: process.env.PERSONIZE_SECRET_KEY! });

async function generateSolutionPageCopy(visitorEmail: string, solutionPageSlug: string) {
    // Layer 1: Governance -- fetch brand rules and messaging playbook
    const governance = await client.ai.smartGuidelines({
        message: `brand voice, approved claims, messaging guidelines for ${solutionPageSlug} solution page, industry-specific proof points, competitor mention policy`,
        mode: 'deep',
    });

    // Layer 2: Unified Memory -- assemble cross-source visitor context
    const [visitorDigest, pageHistory] = await Promise.all([
        client.memory.smartDigest({ email: visitorEmail, type: 'Contact', token_budget: 2500 }),
        client.memory.recall({
            query: 'pages viewed, content engagement, signup details, stated goals',
            email: visitorEmail,
        }),
    ]);

    const context = [
        governance.data?.compiledContext,
        visitorDigest.data?.compiledContext,
        pageHistory.data,
    ].filter(Boolean).join('\n\n---\n\n');

    // Layer 3: Multi-step generation
    const result = await client.ai.prompt({
        context,
        instructions: [
            {
                prompt: `Identify: visitor industry, role, company size, journey stage, pages already viewed. What specific facts do we have? What is their most likely pain point related to the "${solutionPageSlug}" solution?`,
                maxSteps: 3,
            },
            {
                prompt: 'Check governance: What claims can we make for their industry? What tone? What proof points? What must we avoid?',
                maxSteps: 2,
            },
            {
                prompt: 'Generate solution page copy as JSON: { "headline": "...", "subheadline": "...", "valueProp": "... (2-3 sentences referencing their specific situation)", "useCaseExample": "...", "ctaText": "...", "ctaSubtext": "..." }. Every field must be tailored to this visitor. Do not use generic phrases like "modern teams" or "powerful platform." Reference specific facts from memory.',
                maxSteps: 5,
            },
        ],
        evaluate: true,
        evaluationCriteria: 'Content must: (1) reference at least 2 specific facts about the visitor, (2) follow brand voice from governance, (3) make no unapproved claims, (4) feel written for one person not a segment.',
    });

    return result.data;
}
```

## Proposal 2: AI-Generated Blog CTAs and Content Bridges

### The Experience

Your blog posts currently end with the same generic CTA for every reader. With Personize, the CTA section at the bottom of each post (and inline CTAs) is **generated per reader** -- the AI writes a bridge paragraph connecting the blog topic to the reader's specific situation, then generates a CTA that references what they actually care about.

### Why It Needs All Three Layers

- **Governance:** Rules about what CTAs are appropriate for each lifecycle stage (a lead gets a different CTA than an existing customer), approved calls to action, tone guidelines for blog content.
- **Memory:** The reader's role, company, lifecycle stage from HubSpot, which other blog posts they have read, which solution pages they have visited, and whether they are already a customer or a prospect.
- **Generation:** AI WRITES a unique bridge paragraph. Not "If industry = healthcare, show CTA variant 3" but a composed paragraph that ties the blog content to the reader's actual context.

### Before

Every reader of your blog post "5 Ways to Reduce Customer Churn" sees:

> "Ready to reduce churn? Try our platform free for 14 days. [Start Free Trial]"

### After

A CS manager at a mid-market SaaS company who has already signed up for a trial sees:

> "You are already in your trial -- and with 200+ contacts in your account, you have the data to put these churn-reduction strategies into practice right now. The Health Score dashboard (Settings > Integrations > Health Score) can flag at-risk accounts using the signals from this article. [Set Up Health Scoring in Your Trial]"

A VP of Sales at an enterprise company who has never signed up sees:

> "Your team manages 150+ enterprise accounts. The difference between catching a churn signal at day 30 vs. day 90 is the difference between a save conversation and a loss post-mortem. Here is how sales and CS teams at companies your size operationalize these five strategies without adding headcount. [See the Enterprise Retention Playbook]"

### Technical Pattern

```typescript
async function generateBlogCTA(visitorEmail: string, blogPostSlug: string, blogPostTopic: string) {
    const governance = await client.ai.smartGuidelines({
        message: 'blog CTA guidelines, lifecycle-stage-appropriate calls to action, brand voice for content marketing',
        mode: 'fast',
    });

    const visitorContext = await client.memory.smartDigest({
        email: visitorEmail, type: 'Contact', token_budget: 2000,
    });

    const context = [
        governance.data?.compiledContext,
        visitorContext.data?.compiledContext,
        `Current blog post: "${blogPostSlug}" about "${blogPostTopic}"`,
    ].filter(Boolean).join('\n\n---\n\n');

    const result = await client.ai.prompt({
        context,
        instructions: [
            {
                prompt: 'Analyze: Who is this reader? What lifecycle stage (prospect/trial/customer)? What do they care about based on their history? How does this blog topic connect to their situation?',
                maxSteps: 3,
            },
            {
                prompt: 'Check governance: What CTA is appropriate for their lifecycle stage? What tone for blog CTAs? What links are approved?',
                maxSteps: 2,
            },
            {
                prompt: 'Generate as JSON: { "bridgeParagraph": "... (2-3 sentences connecting blog topic to their situation)", "ctaText": "...", "ctaUrl": "https://...", "ctaSubtext": "..." }. The bridge paragraph must reference a specific fact about the reader AND connect it to the blog content.',
                maxSteps: 5,
            },
        ],
        evaluate: true,
    });

    return result.data;
}
```

## Proposal 3: AI-Generated Landing Page Copy Per Visitor

### The Experience

When a visitor arrives at a landing page -- from an ad, an email campaign, or an organic search -- the page copy is **generated for them**. The headline, hero paragraph, social proof, and CTA are all written by AI using their HubSpot data, browsing history, and your messaging guidelines.

### Why It Needs All Three Layers

- **Governance:** Approved claims and messaging angles per audience, compliance rules (especially if you serve regulated industries), brand voice guidelines, and which case studies / proof points to reference for which verticals.
- **Memory:** Referral source (which ad or email brought them), HubSpot lifecycle stage, company and role, previous page views on your site, any past interactions with sales.
- **Generation:** AI WRITES the landing page copy per visitor. Not segment-based A/B variants but truly unique copy. A healthcare visitor and a fintech visitor see entirely different pages, and two healthcare visitors at different journey stages also see different pages.

### Before

Everyone who clicks your Google Ad for "customer data platform" lands on:

> "The All-in-One Customer Data Platform. Unify your data. Personalize every interaction. [Get Started Free]"

### After

A product manager at a healthcare company who clicked the ad sees:

> "Patient Communication That Knows the Full Story. Your care coordination team juggles EHR data, patient surveys, and support interactions across disconnected tools. Here is how healthcare product teams unify patient context into a single memory layer -- HIPAA-compliant, API-first, and built for teams that ship fast. [See the Healthcare Quickstart]"

A developer at a B2B SaaS company who arrived from an email about API personalization sees:

> "Ship Personalization in Days, Not Quarters. You have user data in HubSpot, usage analytics in Mixpanel, and support tickets in Intercom. Right now, none of your features can see all three at once. Three SDK calls change that: memorize, recall, generate. [Read the TypeScript Quickstart]"

### Technical Pattern

            },

        evaluate: true,
        evaluationCriteria: 'Copy must: reference the visitor\'s industry or role specifically, make only governance-approved claims, connect the referral context to the value proposition, and feel written for one person.',
    });

    return result.data;
}

## Proposal 4: AI-Written Post-Signup Onboarding Narrative

### The Experience

After someone signs up, instead of a generic "Welcome! Here are 5 steps to get started" page, they see an onboarding guide **written for them** -- referencing their role, their company's tech stack, their likely goals based on which solution pages they viewed before signing up, and the setup steps that matter most for their situation.

### Why It Needs All Three Layers

- **Governance:** Onboarding best practices, what to emphasize for each ICP segment, product messaging rules, approved integrations to suggest.
- **Memory:** Signup form data + HubSpot enrichment + pre-signup page views (they viewed 4 API-related solution pages, so they care about integrations) + any sales conversations.
- **Generation:** AI WRITES a multi-paragraph guide with personalized steps. Not "choose your path: developer / marketer / manager" but a guide that already knows their path and writes accordingly.

### Before

> "Welcome to [Product]! Here is how to get started:
> 1. Connect your CRM
> 2. Import your contacts
> 3. Set up your first automation
> 4. Invite your team
> 5. Explore the dashboard"

> "Welcome, Sarah. Based on your interest in email personalization and your team's HubSpot setup, here is your personalized quickstart:
>
> **Step 1: Connect HubSpot** (the integration you were reading about). This pulls in your contacts, companies, and deal stages -- takes about 3 minutes. Your 2,400 HubSpot contacts will sync automatically.
>
> **Step 2: Set up your brand voice.** Before generating any personalized emails, define how your company sounds. This ensures every email the AI writes matches your team's tone -- whether it is going to a prospect or an existing customer.

> **Step 3: Generate your first personalized email.** Pick one contact from your pipeline. The AI will write an email using their full HubSpot history plus your brand rules. Compare it to what you would have written manually.

## Proposal 5: AI-Composed Nurture Emails Based on Page View Behavior

### The Experience

When a visitor views specific solution pages but does not sign up, they receive a follow-up email **written by AI** that references the exact pages they visited, connects those topics to their company's likely challenges, and offers a relevant next step -- all governed by your email playbook and brand voice.

### Why It Needs All Three Layers

- **Governance:** Email playbook (frequency limits, tone, approved CTAs per lifecycle stage), brand voice, what can be referenced about the visitor's behavior (page views: fine; time-on-page: feels creepy), compliance (CAN-SPAM).
- **Memory:** HubSpot CRM data (company, role, deal stage) + page view history (they viewed the Security, API, and Pricing pages in that order -- which tells a story) + any prior email engagement + enrichment data.
- **Generation:** AI WRITES the email from scratch. Not a drip sequence template with merge fields but a composed message that synthesizes their browsing pattern into a narrative.

### Before

Everyone who views 3+ pages without signing up gets:

> "Hi {first_name}, noticed you have been checking us out. Ready to take the next step? [Start Free Trial]"

### After

A CTO at a 100-person fintech company who viewed Security, API Docs, and Pricing (in that order) gets:

> "Hi James -- you spent time on our security architecture and API documentation before looking at pricing, which tells me compliance and integration depth matter more than feature lists for your evaluation.
>
> Two things that might help: (1) Our SOC2 Type II report and penetration test results are available under NDA -- happy to share them. (2) Our API supports the webhook signing and audit logging patterns that regulated fintech teams typically require.
>
> If you are evaluating us for a team of ~100, the Pro tier is likely the right fit for your API volume. Worth a 20-minute walkthrough? [Schedule a Technical Demo]"

### Technical Pattern

```typescript
async function generateBehaviorNurtureEmail(visitorEmail: string, pagesViewed: string[]) {
    const governance = await client.ai.smartGuidelines({
        message: 'email nurture playbook, behavior-based follow-up guidelines, brand voice, data reference policy (what visitor behavior is OK to mention vs feels intrusive), CAN-SPAM compliance',
        mode: 'deep',
    });

    const visitorDigest = await client.memory.smartDigest({
        email: visitorEmail, type: 'Contact', token_budget: 2500,
    });

    const context = [
        governance.data?.compiledContext,
        visitorDigest.data?.compiledContext,
        `Pages viewed (in order): ${pagesViewed.join(' -> ')}`,
    ].filter(Boolean).join('\n\n---\n\n');

    const result = await client.ai.prompt({
        context,
        instructions: [
            {
                prompt: 'Analyze the page view sequence. What story does it tell about the visitor\'s priorities? What are they evaluating? What concern or goal does the browsing pattern reveal?',
                maxSteps: 3,
            },
            {
                prompt: 'Check email governance: What behavior is OK to reference explicitly? What feels intrusive? What CTA is appropriate for their lifecycle stage? What tone?',
                maxSteps: 2,
            },
            {
                prompt: 'Write a follow-up email. Lead with an insight from their browsing pattern (without being creepy). Connect to a specific resource or next step relevant to their evaluation. Under 150 words. Output SUBJECT: and BODY_HTML: as separate fields.',
                maxSteps: 5,
            },
        ],
        evaluate: true,
        evaluationCriteria: 'Email must: (1) reference browsing behavior without feeling invasive, (2) provide a genuinely useful next step, (3) follow brand voice, (4) be under 150 words, (5) have separate subject and body.',
    });

    // Close the feedback loop
    await client.memory.memorize({
        content: `[NURTURE-EMAIL] Sent behavior-based follow-up. Pages referenced: ${pagesViewed.join(', ')}. Subject: ${String(result.data).match(/SUBJECT:\s*(.+)/i)?.[1] || 'N/A'}`,
        email: visitorEmail,
        enhanced: true,
        tags: ['generated', 'nurture', 'behavior-triggered'],
    });

    return result.data;
}
```

## Governance You Should Set Up First

Before generating any content, create these governance variables. They ensure every AI-generated paragraph across all five surfaces speaks with one voice and follows your rules:

| Variable | What It Controls | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `brand-voice` | Tone and style across all surfaces | "Professional but direct. No buzzwords ('synergy', 'leverage', 'ecosystem'). Use 'you' and 'your', not 'users' and 'customers'. Technical when talking to developers, plain language for business roles." |
| `messaging-playbook` | Approved claims and angles per ICP | "Enterprise: lead with security, compliance, scale. SMB: lead with speed, simplicity, ROI. Startup: lead with developer experience and time-to-value." |
| `competitor-policy` | How to handle competitor mentions | "Never disparage by name. If a visitor viewed competitor comparison pages, acknowledge the evaluation and focus on differentiators." |
| `data-reference-policy` | What visitor behavior is OK to mention | "Can reference: solution pages viewed, blog posts read, stated goals from signup. Cannot reference: exact time spent on pages, scroll depth, number of visits (feels surveillance-like)." |
| `cta-rules` | CTA appropriateness per lifecycle stage | "Prospect: 'See how it works' or 'Read the guide'. Trial user: 'Set up X in your account'. Customer: 'Talk to your account manager'. Never use 'Buy now' or 'Don't miss out'." |

## Data You Need to Memorize

You already have the data. Here is how to feed it into Personize's unified memory so the AI can use it all at once:

| Source | What to Memorize | Method | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| **HubSpot CRM** | Contacts, companies, deals, lifecycle stages, lead scores | `memorizeBatch()` | Daily sync or webhook-triggered |
| **Page Views** | Solution pages viewed, blog posts read, landing page visits (with timestamps and order) | `memorize()` per session | Real-time or batched hourly |
| **Signup Data** | Form fields: role, company, goals, use case | `memorize()` on signup event | On event |
| **Email Engagement** | Opens, clicks, replies to your campaigns | `memorize()` per campaign | Post-send batch |
| **Generated Content** | Every AI-generated email, CTA, or page copy that was delivered | `memorize()` after delivery | On delivery (closes the feedback loop) |

## Where to Start

**Week 1:** Memorize your HubSpot contacts and companies via `memorizeBatch()`. Set up brand voice and messaging playbook governance variables. Generate your first personalized solution page copy for a handful of known visitors and compare it to your static copy.

**Week 2:** Wire solution page personalization to your 5 highest-traffic solution pages. Set up page view memorization so the AI starts accumulating browsing context.

**Week 3-4:** Add blog CTA personalization and behavior-based nurture emails. Connect landing page generation for your top campaign pages. Set up the feedback loop (memorize every generated piece of content after delivery).

**Ongoing:** Expand to all 53 solution pages. Add post-signup onboarding narrative. Review governance variables monthly and update based on what the AI is generating.

The key advantage across all of this: one set of governance rules, one unified memory, and original AI-written content at every touchpoint -- not 53 sets of A/B test variants maintained by hand.
